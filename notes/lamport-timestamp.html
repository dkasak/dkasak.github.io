Lamport timestamps

#distributed systems #algorithms #causality #partial ordering

2019-11-28 – Denis Kasak

A Lamport timestamp (or a Lamport clock) is a simple and lightweight synchronization algorithm between two independent systems. It provides a partial ordering of events which preserves causality.

If an event \(A\) could have influenced another event \(B\) causally, it is said to have happened-before (\(A\) happened-before \(B\)). For such events, we have the guarantee that the timestamp related to \(A\), that is \(C(A)\), will be less than \(C(B)\), the Lamport timestamp of \(B\).

That is

\[ A \operatorname{happened-before} B \implies C(A) < C(B) \]

C.f. vector clocks, a generalization of this method from two to many independent systems.
